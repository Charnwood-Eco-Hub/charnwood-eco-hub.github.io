# Summer 2026 Newsletter

**Welcome to the Summer 2026 Edition of the Charnwood Eco Hub Newsletter!**

Summer is here and it’s shaping up to be a creative if sultry
season at the Eco Hub, sadly due in no small part to the effects of
climate change. We welcomed teachers and educators to the hub recently
to learn about what we can offer schools, and we have started our
summer season tour of the local fetes and fairs -  we've already been
to Bradgate Park Spring Fair, but we will also be running activities
at Loughborough Mela, Loughborough Pride and Syston Summer Fair.

We’ve got some fun [workshops](/workshops-and-events) coming up, including [handmade
book-making](/workshops-and-events/handmade-book-workshop-jun26/), [upcycled terrariums](/workshops-and-events/upcycled-terrarium-workshop-jul26/), [bunting-making](/workshops-and-events/bunting-workshop-aug26/), [screenprinting](/workshops-and-events/screenprinting-workshop-jul26/) and more.   

We also have some very exciting news (see below), and as always, thank
you for supporting the Eco Hub and helping build a more sustainable
community. 🌱

# 4th Annual Sustainability Fair

![Sustainability Fair photo montage](/assets/img/susfair26-montage.jpg)

The 4th Annual Charnwood Sustainability Fair was another
tremendous success for Charnwood Eco Hub, bringing together hundreds
of people for a day dedicated to sustainability, community action and
positive environmental change.As organisers of the event, we were
delighted to welcome a diverse range of local businesses, charities,
community groups and environmental organisations, all showcasing
practical ways to live more sustainably. Visitors had the opportunity
to explore stalls, take part in activities, learn new skills and
discover the many projects helping to build a greener future across
Charnwood and beyond.

The Fair has become a flagship event in the local sustainability
calendar, and this year's event demonstrated just how much interest
there is in reducing waste, protecting the environment and supporting
community-led solutions. The atmosphere throughout the day was
positive, inspiring and full of meaningful conversations and
connections.

A huge thank you goes to all of our exhibitors, volunteers,
performers, speakers and visitors who helped make the event possible.
In particular, we are grateful to the volunteers who worked tirelessly
behind the scenes before, during and after the event to ensure
everything ran smoothly.  Now in its fourth year, the Charnwood
Sustainability Fair continues to grow in both size and impact. It
reflects Charnwood Eco Hub's commitment to making sustainability
accessible, practical and engaging for everyone, and we are incredibly
proud of what the event has become. We are already looking forward to
bringing the community together again next year.

# We're Moving!

![Moving Home poster](/assets/img/new-home-summer26.png)

We are delighted to announce that Charnwood Eco Hub will soon be
relocating to temporary new premises at 7–8 Baxtergate, kindly
provided by Loughborough Baptist Church.

Over the next few weeks, we'll be busy packing, moving and setting up
the new space, with an opening date to be confirmed soon. The move
will allow us to continue delivering our Scrapstore, creative
upcycling workshops, refill station, Library of Things and community
projects while we work towards securing a permanent home for the Eco
Hub. We also hope to open finally open the Makerspace in our temporary
new home, and offer some new membership options.

As we prepare for this next chapter, we would like to extend our
sincere thanks to SOFA for hosting us over the past 18 months. Their
support has played a crucial role in helping the Eco Hub grow from an
idea into a thriving community organisation. We are incredibly
grateful for the space, encouragement and friendship they have
provided throughout our time there.  We would also like to thank
Loughborough Baptist Church for generously offering us a temporary
home at 7–8 Baxtergate. Their support will enable us to continue
serving the community, running events and developing new projects
during this important period of transition.

While this is a temporary move, it represents an exciting step
forward for Charnwood Eco Hub. We look forward to welcoming you to our
new town centre location very soon and sharing updates as we settle
into our new home.  Watch this space for opening details and
opportunities to get involved!

# People's Emergency Briefing

![People's Emergency Briefing poster](/assets/img/peoples-emergency-briefing.jpg)

More than 80 people attended the People's Emergency Briefing
screening in Loughborough on Friday night including our local MP and a
number of local Councillors from Charnwood Borough Council and
Leicestershire County Council.The film ended with stunned silence in
the room. The climate crisis is a lot to face up to. But we then had a
brilliant conversation about people's thoughts on what we do next
locally. Between us, we have what it takes to tackle this problem and
create a bright future we can all thrive in.

Popular ideas included lobbying our local MP to ensure government
takes climate change seriously and local, practical projects to tackle
climate change.

Want to put on your own screening? Find out more at [https://www.nebriefing.org/host-the-film](https://www.nebriefing.org/host-the-film)

The event was organised by Loughborough Climate Vigil, Charnwood Eco
Hub Transition Loughborough Barrow Greener Living Incredible Edible
Loughborough, Parents for the Future Leicestershire and Communities
Foundation Network to make this happen.

# New Project Launch - The Leicestershire Fixers' Toolkit

![Leicestershire Fixers Toolkit poster](/assets/img/leicestershire-fixers-toolkit.jpg)

We're excited to learn of the new Leicestershire Fixers Toolkit, a
county-wide project funded by the National Lottery Community Fund and
delivered by Leicestershire County Council. The initiative aims to
support repair cafés and community fixer groups across
Leicestershire, helping them grow, connect and save even more items
from going to waste.

Over the next 12 months, the project will develop an online
toolkit packed with practical resources, training opportunities and
guidance for both existing repair groups and communities interested in
starting their own repair events. The project will also include access
to a shared tool library to help groups get established and thrive.

Repair groups play a vital role in reducing waste, saving people
money and bringing communities together. As an organisation committed
to reuse, repair and the circular economy, Charnwood Eco Hub is
delighted to see this investment in Leicestershire's growing repair
movement. We look forward to seeing how the project develops and the
opportunities it creates for communities across the county. Find out
more [here](https://www.lesswaste.org.uk/2026/06/10/new-project-launch-the-leicestershire-fixers-toolkit)

# Biodiversity Literacy Training

![Biodiversity Literacy Training poster](/assets/img/biodiversity-literacy-training.png)

Biodiversity Literacy Training is an engaging and interactive
online training developed by the University of Edinburgh.  As part of
Loughborough University's commitment to biodiversity and the
community, the University Sustainability team are offering community
members free places on the course (it would usually cost £15 for
certification). The next date is Thursday 2nd July.

If community members are interested and would like to take the
course, all they have to do is email [m.ward@lboro.ac.uk](mailto:m.ward@lboro.ac.uk)
with their postcode and short description on your reasons for signing up.

# Refill Station

![Refill Station poster](/assets/img/refill-station-containers.jpg)

Did you know you can reduce packaging waste and save money by
using our refill station? Bring your own containers and stock up on a
range of household and personal care essentials, from laundry liquid
and washing-up liquid to shampoo and more. By refilling instead of
buying new bottles, you're helping to cut down on single-use plastic
while supporting a more sustainable way of shopping. Pop in and give
it a try – every refill makes a difference.  We are hoping to widen
our refill range once we are in our new home.
